Get to Know the HBV Vaccine

The Hepatitis B virus was first developed in 1965 by Dr. Baruch Blumberg, and the first vaccine for HBV was developed 4 years later (1). It initially contained a heat treated form of the virus. The first commercially available HBV vaccine was available in 1981, which contained inactivated virus strains from blood donors. From 1986 forward, the HBV vaccine was created through DNA recombination which does not contain any blood products (1). This is the current vaccine that is used today. It is currently impossible to get HBV as a result of the vaccine due to recombinant techniques (2). The HBV vaccine does not cause HBV, autism, or any other chronic disorders (3). The HBV vaccine is considered to be the “safest and most effective vaccine ever produced” and is readily available (3). Serious side effects are very uncommon (4). There are 2-dose, 3-dose, or 4-dose vaccination series (5). Prevent you and your children from deadly HBV by getting a vaccine today.
